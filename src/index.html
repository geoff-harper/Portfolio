Yellow Pages Onboarding Questionnaire

The Yellow Pages onboarding questionnaire was a project I volunteered for as it was the first MV* project I did with any sense of scale. New clients are sent this form to expedite the process of nailing down what they’re looking for and what they have. I developed this with Vue.js and a small PHP mailing library. I also co-designed. I definitely felt the challenge here with a non-trivial (read: 600+) different products and services options, among other things. It was certainly an enlightening (read: annoying) experience trying to configure the server to send mail properly as well. While it’s a candidate for some refactoring, it fills the need and is a massive improvement over the former process.

SearchBars

SearchBars was a fun side project I came up with for the purpose of learning React. The premise of the web app is to be able to search for bars that serve your favourite craft beer or for your favourite bar’s tap list. Unfortunately, once I got the functionality down with 3 bars in my area and added a few more, I realized that fleshing out the list (and then maintaining it) would be a staggering time commitment for a fun side project. Maybe one day someone will create a Vancouver bar scene API. Regardless, this was a good introduction to rapidly evolving MV* scene.

Yellow Pages Site Viewer

This website previewer was something I developed in my spare time at Yellow Pages. Its purpose is to compile screenshots of some sites we created. This was done with vanilla CSS and Javascript so no particularly fancy tech was used. I wanted the aesthetic to simulate flipping through a box of vinyls which I tried to achieve with 3D transforms and box shadows. All of the previews themselves were designed and built by myself on our proprietary WYSIWYG platform. It should be noted that all the previews are drafts and belong to Yellow Pages.
